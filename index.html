Welcome to my personal web page!

My name is Predrag Filipovikj. Currently, I am employed at Scania AB CV as a Test Method Developer. I am working in the comple vehicle integration testing team, and my main responsibility is to improve the verification methods used to date for hardware- and solftware in the loop (HIL/SIL) verifiction of our product.

In spite of the fact that I am working in industry now, I am still very passionate about research. My main research interest is the board topic of formal verification of embedded software. I am particularly interested in the various kinds of model-checking based formal verification, be it symbolic model checking, statistical model checking, probabilistic model checking, or SAT/SMT-based model checking. My research is mainly focused on developing methods and tools based on the aforementioned techniques that can be applied in industrial settings.

At the moment, I am mostly focused on system integration testing techniques, for performing Hardware- and Software-In-the-Loop testing.

If you are student who is looking for a thesis project within the broad area of software verification (formal verification or testing) please do not hesitate to get in touch.

I have published more than 10 peer-reviewed scientific publications and several technical reports. The complete list of publications is available here, but you can also check out my Google Scholar profile. I am also contributing to the dissemination of my research results through various tools. I have developed, either myself or with other developers the following tools and libraries:

  • SIMPPAAL: A tool for statistical model checkig of Simulink models.
  • ProPAS: A tool for formal requirements specification using specification patterns.
  • SYMC: A tool for bounded model checking of Simulink/Stateflow programs.
  • SESf: A library for generating a transition relation for Stateflow programs.
  • J2Uppaal: A JAVA API for easy manipulation of UPPAAL models.

All of the above listed tools are free and open source. Each tool is liked to its own Github project repository. For more projects, please visit my GitHub profile.
